About

A highly motivated second-year computer science student at the University of Adelaide, Brandon Smart is passionate about solving interesting challenges in computer graphics and algorithmics.

Brandon has a strong desire to create high quality work and projects, achieving a perfect GPA of 7.0 in his Bachelor of Computer Science (Advanced) degree. He also values being challenged and working with interesting people, currently acting as secretary of the Adelaide Competitive Programming Club and placing third in South Australia with his team in the ICPC programming competition.

In addition to his studies, Brandon is highly motivated to learn new skills through the development of side projects, exploring computer graphics and data science through the creation of interactive webpages, and volunteering to lead programming workshops throughout high school and university. Brandon is skilled in analysing problems and finding creative and novel solutions, desiring to work in an engaging environment where he can solve interesting and dynamic problems with others.

2018 Projects

Eurovision Data Analysis

A series of interactive visualisations created to explore voting patterns in the Eurovision Song Contest. It explores whether systematic bias exists in Eurovision, analysing whether language and geographical location have a strong effect of how countries vote. Created with WebGL, Javascript and HTML/CSS.

Reddit Post Gallery

A program that displays the saved and upvoted Reddit posts of the user as a gallery categorised by subreddit. Also allows for the output of markdown files listing the saved/upvoted posts. Built using Python, HTML/CSS, Javascript, the Reddit API and PRAW. Temporarily unavailable due to API constraints.


2017 Projects

TravelRate

A web and database development project designed to allow users to view and rate locations around the world. Utilises PHP, HTML/CSS, SQLLite and the RedBean library to allow users to add locations and reviews, with additional data processing done in PHP. Featuring PDF and histogram generation for reviews. No longer available online to view.
